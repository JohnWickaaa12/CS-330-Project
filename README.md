# CS 330 – Computational Graphics and Visualization  
## Final Project Portfolio Submission  

This repository contains my final project for CS 330, where I created a 3D scene using OpenGL. The goal of this project was to demonstrate my 
understanding of computational graphics and visualization, including object modeling, transformations, lighting, and textures.

## Reflection

### How do I approach designing software?

When I design software, I try to keep things simple at the start and then build on top of that. For this project, I broke the scene down 
into basic shapes instead of trying to make everything detailed right away. That made it easier to get something working first and then 
improve it over time. I focus on understanding what I’m building before worrying about making it look perfect.

### What new design skills has your work on the project helped you to craft?

One of the biggest skills I developed was thinking in 3D space. I learned how objects are positioned, scaled, and rotated to create a scene. 
I also got better at simplifying designs by combining basic shapes instead of overcomplicating objects. Another skill I improved was 
understanding how lighting and textures affect realism.

### What design process did you follow for your project work?

I followed an iterative design process. I started with a basic version of the scene and gradually added more objects, lighting, and textures. 
Each milestone allowed me to improve a specific part of the project. Instead of doing everything at once, I worked in smaller steps and tested 
along the way.

### How could tactics from your design approach be applied in future work?

The main tactic I would apply in the future is starting simple and building up. Breaking projects into smaller parts makes them easier 
to manage and improves overall quality. This approach can be used in almost any software development project.

---

### How do I approach developing programs?

When developing programs, I start by getting a basic version working first, even if it’s not perfect. Then I go back and improve it. 
I also test frequently instead of waiting until the end to fix issues.

### What new development strategies did you use while working on your 3D scene?

I used transformations like scaling, rotation, and positioning to control objects in the scene. I also focused more on organizing my code 
so it could handle multiple objects and textures more efficiently.

### How did iteration factor into your development?

Iteration played a major role in this project. I continuously adjusted object placement, lighting, and proportions to improve the scene. 
Each version helped me understand what worked and what needed improvement.

### How has your approach to developing code evolved throughout the milestones?

At the beginning, I focused on just getting objects to appear on the screen. As I progressed, I started paying more attention to 
structure, readability, and how everything worked together. By the end, I felt more confident making changes and improving the design.

### How can computer science help me in reaching my goals?

Computer science is directly related to my goal of becoming a software engineer. Projects like this help me build real-world skills, 
especially in problem-solving and system design.

### How do computational graphics and visualizations give you new knowledge and skills for your educational pathway?

This project helped me understand how math, logic, and programming work together to create visual outputs. It also gave me a stronger 
foundation for future courses that involve graphics, simulations, or more advanced programming concepts.

### How do computational graphics and visualizations give you new knowledge and skills for your professional pathway?

This project gave me experience working through complex problems step by step. I learned how rendering, transformations, and lighting 
work together, which can apply to areas like game development, simulations, and other technical software fields.
